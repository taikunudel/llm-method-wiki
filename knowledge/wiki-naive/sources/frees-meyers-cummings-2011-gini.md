## Summary
This paper by Edward W. Frees, Glenn Meyers, and A. David Cummings extends the concepts of the Lorenz curve and the Gini index to the financial context by ordering risks using risk-based scores relative to prices. It offers a framework to cope with adverse selection and measures potential profit using the Gini index. The paper provides examples using homeowners insurance and explores the statistical properties of the Gini index.

## Key Claims
- The [[GiniIndex]] and [[LorenzCurve]] can be extended to evaluate and manage risks in financial contexts, including insurance.
- The ordered Lorenz curve uses "relativities" to better compare premiums to losses, addressing issues of adverse selection.
- The Gini index is linked to economic profit measures and can identify profitable insurance portfolios.

## Key Quotes
