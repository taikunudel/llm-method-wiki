## Definition
Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) is a class of algorithms for sampling from probability distributions based on constructing a Markov chain. It is primarily used in Bayesian inference for approximating complex posterior distributions.

## Applications
- Utilized extensively in [[Bayesian methods]] for parameter estimation and model inference.
- Applied in [[TweedieDistribution]] analyses, particularly in data with significant variance structure.

## Connections
- Related to [[MonteCarloEM]], both methods employ stochastic processes for statistical inference.
- Critical for Bayesian modeling frameworks where exact analytical solutions are unattainable.
